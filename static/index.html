Portfolio

Below is a selection of projects we've recently worked on.

Geofolio is a small project with a big vision: to make the world's environmental data accessible and understandable for everyone (2019-present).

Environmental data is notoriously difficult to use if you're not an expert. It gets hidden away in unusable "data portals" and without some serious GIS skills it is difficult to use this data for your own purposes.

Geofolio simplifies this. It has only one step: Select an area on Earth that you're interested in. It will then automatically generate a factsheet for you about that area containing basic information about land cover, elevation, hydrology, soils, and crops. All data is downloadable in accessible GIS formats for further exploration.

https://geofolio.org/

Using Open Geodata to Detect Deforestation and Land Use Change Hotspots (2021).

Together with the SourceUp platform at IDH we developed a methodology to detect deforestation and land use change hotspots based on data by Global Forest Watch, Mapbiomas, Copernicus Land Cover Products, and Planet/NICFI satellite imagery.

A PDF report is generated with the hotspots. For each hotspot sattelite imagery from Planet is collected from just before and after the change for verification and validation purposes.

https://www.idhsustainabletrade.com/

Snapspace is a simple collaboration space for groups (2018-2020).

Snapspace was a departure from applications built on geodata, and is a simple ephemeral online collaboration space for groups, where you could easily invite other members across different organizations and share documents, images, messages, and events.

Unfortunately Snapspace never gained any traction and has stopped accepting new signups since 2020.

https://www.snapspace.io/
